I want to vent a bit. Ever since i am getting more and more ingulfed into daily AI usage, and moreover into reflecting on the meaning of its high capabilities, i connect it to the role of human intelligence and sense of belonging.

## Do artificial neurons feel themselves?

A simplification, or better a starting point of my reflection is that an AI does not have a body. This goes merely over the concept of attaching the parts of a body, like a robot, to an AI, but to the deeper concept of actually exisiting in and inhabitating that body. This is a point of the research on consciousness, and a part that at the moment seems impossible to replicate artificially.

In animal bodies, a reaction to an external stressor provocates reactions of various type, fight or flight for instance,  which then can get ingrained into neural pathways and in neurophisiological configurations. This can lead to a behavioural modification, which is also supported by a change in the cognitive process. While behaviour and cognition can potentially be included in the code and in the artificial neurons, the essence of feeling, the one that in real beings produces such modifications, is still a potential mistery, and the key to my questions. If there is a way to understand at a deep systemic level how to feel oneself in danger, safe or in love, then this can potentially be made into an artificial product, or even modified on a human. Would it even be moral to do the latter?

## AI producing human burnout

Why something that does not even know of their existence as a physical entity can rob the intellectual functions of real sentient beings? Continous replicas of human output are more and more common. Is human intelligence still useful as before? How can we keep up with more and more and information being thrown at us, without the ability to analyze and discern what is useful for us only because the quantity of it has become more than what we can possibly tolerate?

_(Disclaimer, original post was written in 2021)_

In Andrei Tarkovsky's film [Nostalghia](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-gH1cprEg0w), a Russian poet is asked if it is possible to understand anything about Italian culture by reading the works of Dante or Macchiavelli. The poet's cold and harsh answer is that we could understand each other only by abolishing the borders between states. The concept of the film resonated with me, and this article is the resulting interpretation.

I am extremely intrigued by the idea that we can get rid of any prejudice or preconception. This cannot be reduced to the scientific search, for an ultimate truth or whatever you use to call it, the theory of everything. Or in more philosophical terms, a tear in the veil of Maya. I mean something conceptually more humble, that is, trying to see one's way of thinking from an angle outside one's common experience. It seems to me that this is an extremely fruitful attitude in research activity, but I think it is a principle that can be applied in a multitude of situations.

I would like to connect this thought to the concept of the information bubble. In practical terms, the environment, people, cultural class, but also more violently, the profiling done on social networks, influences and categorizes the information we receive on a daily basis. And in turn these have an important effect in the way of thinking, characterizing ideological categories. This leads to the aforementioned phenomenon, namely the bubble, which only reinforces certain judgments that we are used to applying, and to discarding, or simply making unknown distant ways of thinking that we are used to.

Because of the presence of the bubble, I have a fear that the ideas that circulate may be biased, and therefore one may be influenced by them. This results in ignoring important details because of one's past and the information that has been assimilated. One can perceive on numerous occasions that many of the people with whom one interacts are certainly subject to this phenomenon. The intellectual effort required to realize the presence of one's bubble is considerable.

It would be nice to have a top-down view in many matters that grip us. That would mean getting rid of the bubble. Obviously a view from above is not always the ideal solution, but I think it is important, and in general I observe more often the opposite problem. Wanting to get out of the box is that for me. That is, getting rid of the burden of one's identity, and even being able to get to look at the issue from the outside, to understand what boxes enclose thoughts and categorize them, and being able to express oneself outside of them.

The expression “Thinking outside the box” is in common use in the English language. It is used for logical questions, where the solution lies outside a set of rules that seem to have been provided, but are really just our box. A famous example, which gave this concept its very name, is [the nine-dot problem](https://www.artofplay.com/blogs/stories/history-of-the-nine-dot-problem), in which you have to touch 9 dots situated in a 3×3 grid with 4 continuous lines. One way to solve it is to extend the line beyond the grid, that is, by literally stepping outside the box.

Some philosophies believe that there is a world spirit, and that each of us is a part of this spirit, and that [in reincarnation we become another part of ourselves](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h6fcK_fRYaI). That is, we could reincarnate into any person who has existed and who will exist. And we ourselves therefore are the result of this continuous process of reincarnation. In a sense this leads to the idea that coming out of the box is a natural continuation, and that the boxes are just a distortion of a condition of spiritual oneness. These theories are not really scientific, as they are totally metaphysical. But the meaning I would like to convey from this concept is the unity of the spirit of the world. From a purely evolutionary point of view, the common matrix of the human race lies in its genome, which has a fundamental component in characterizing individuals. The common ancestor of all terrestrial life can be thought to exist, and we are only one possibility of its evolution. The unity, or henosis, of the human race, but also of all life, is a very old concept in philosophy, and I think it is now more important than ever to keep it in mind.

I think thinking in terms of stereotypes has the same matrix. The idea of being able to compartamentalize sets of people, and having a definite idea of them is very fascinating. Although I am instead intrigued by the opposite inspiration, that of the most unpredictable people, generally those subtleties that make all people unique. It would seem a contradiction, since all people come from a common matrix that should possess one identity. One can therefore think that it is precisely these extremes of facets and uniqueness that make existence so alive, interesting and magical, and that therefore the unity of spirit does not deny the infinite possibilities of its realization through people.

Extremely, one can then understand all humankind and its cultures in the same way. We have been influenced to categorize ourselves into social or political containers. Some of these are ideologies, or nations. Even culture itself can lead to canning, but the beautiful thing is that it is nourished by admixtures and by contaminations.

So going back to the film Nostalghia, my interpretation of the film corresponds with this concept. The nostalgia that is the matrix of the film is the result of the cultural characteristics of the land, and which are therefore both celebrated but also fought for. This contradiction is what drives the film's character Domenico to madness, until [the extraordinary scene at the capitol](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YSSoihNiV2k), which is the best description of this dilemma.

The conclusion I would like to draw from this is that all of humanity will inevitably face greater challenges than individual nations have been able to fight so far. The economic system is based primarily on the exploitation of natural resources, with the resulting process of processing and trade. Natural resources are not infinite. Therefore, space colonization, which now seems like a mirage, may become a necessity instead. This is just one example, but there may be numerous instances that will bring civilization as we know it to the brink, and I think we are already experiencing a taste of it now because of covid-19. In any case, at that point commercial, military, or religious conflicts will take second place to the survival of humankind.
